What is Racquetball?

Racquetball is a fast-paced indoor sport combining agility, power, and strategy. Played with a racquet and a hollow rubber ball, players compete in singles or doubles to outscore their opponents in dynamic rallies.

History

Racquetball was invented in the 1950s in the United States and quickly grew in popularity worldwide. It combines elements of tennis, squash, and handball, played in an enclosed court with walls used strategically during play.

Since its inception, racquetball has evolved with formalized rules and equipment, becoming a competitive sport recognized internationally and played at clubs and recreational centers across the globe.

Rules of the Game

Racquetball is played on an indoor court where players use racquets to hit a small rubber ball against the front wall. The ball may rebound off the side and back walls but must not hit the floor before striking the front wall.

Players alternate serves, aiming to win rallies by forcing errors or shots that cannot be returned. Matches are typically played to 15 points, with the best of three or five games format.

Ages & Equipment

Racquetball is accessible for all ages, making it a popular choice for youth, adults, and seniors. Youth leagues and beginner programs help players develop skills progressively.

Players use lightweight racquets and wear eye protection to prevent injuries. Appropriate court shoes with good grip are essential for safety and performance during fast movements.
